Volunteering at Quixote Farm

What You Can Do

Volunteers are the heartbeat of our farm, and there's always something meaningful to do. From filling hay nets and doling out morning feed to making sure Diego gets his carrot and Hotey gets his nose scratch, feeding time is one of the most rewarding parts of the day. You might find yourself brushing Mariposa's coat, helping put the horses out to pasture in the morning, or bringing them back into their stalls as the sun goes down.
There's also the quieter work of refreshing water troughs, tidying stalls, and keeping an eye on Ollie, who will absolutely be watching you back. No experience is needed, just a willingness to show up and get your boots dirty.

How to Volunteer

Reach out to us at QuixoteRescue@gmail.com or hit the Contact Us button below.

Just tell us a little about yourself, your availability, and any experience you have with animals, and we'll take it from there!
